What this calculator estimates

This tool estimates whether your gross salary will keep pace with inflation in real after-tax terms. It models salary raises, inflation, payroll deductions and lifestyle cost growth over the projection horizon.

  • Start with your monthly gross salary, then reduce it by taxes and other deductions to get starting purchasing power.
  • Raise amounts are drawn from a normal distribution using your expected raise mean and volatility, and are applied at the chosen frequency.
  • Inflation is also drawn from a normal distribution, producing a cumulative price index that converts nominal salary into real value.
  • Lifestyle spending grows separately at the lifestyle inflation rate to show whether your real salary can cover rising personal costs.
  • The output shows median real salary, the probability of keeping up with starting purchasing power, and the final-year distribution across 5,000 Monte Carlo runs.

Formula Reference

Starting real salary

startReal = monthlySalary × (1 − taxRate − otherDeductions)
  • monthlySalary = gross salary before deductions
  • taxRate + otherDeductions = total payroll burden
  • startReal = after-tax purchasing power at the start

Nominal salary path

nominalₙ = nominalₙ₋₁ × (1 + max(0, Z))
  • Z ~ N(raiseMean, raiseStd)
  • raises apply only on years aligned with raiseFrequency

Inflation index

cpiₙ = cpiₙ₋₁ × (1 + max(0, I))
  • I ~ N(inflationMean, inflationStd)
  • cpi tracks cumulative price inflation over time

Real salary after inflation

realₙ = (nominalₙ / cpiₙ) × (1 − taxRate − otherDeductions)
  • nominalₙ = gross salary after raises
  • cpiₙ = cumulative inflation factor for year n

Lifestyle cost path

lifestyleₙ = startReal × (1 + lifestyleInflation)ⁿ
  • models spending growth relative to purchasing power

Probability of keeping up

prob = count(realₙ ≥ startReal) ÷ runCount
  • final-year real salary is compared to starting real salary
  • runCount = 5,000 Monte Carlo scenarios
