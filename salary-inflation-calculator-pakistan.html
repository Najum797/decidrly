Why a flat percentage raise can still be a pay cut

Pakistan's inflation hasn't been steady — it's swung from a 30.76% annual rate in 2023 down to 3.53% in 2025, then back into double digits through 2026, registering 11.1% year-on-year as of June 2026 after peaking at 11.7% in May. If your salary reviews use a flat percentage each year without checking it against the actual inflation rate for that specific period, you can end up with a raise that doesn't even keep pace with rising prices — a real-terms pay cut disguised as a raise.

Your personal inflation rate may not match the headline number

The 11.1% figure is a national average across a standard basket of goods. Your actual cost-of-living increase depends on your specific spending mix. As of the latest PBS data, housing and utilities costs rose by 15.5% year-on-year — well above headline inflation — while transport costs have also run persistently high due to elevated energy prices. If rent, electricity, or commuting make up a larger share of your budget than the average household, your personal erosion rate is likely higher than 11.1%, not lower.

Frequently asked questions

How much has inflation eroded salaries in Pakistan?
It depends heavily on the period measured. Annual inflation was as high as 30.76% in 2023, eased to 3.53% in 2025, then climbed back into double digits through 2026, running at 11.1% year-on-year in June 2026. A salary that hasn't risen at least in line with these swings has lost real purchasing power — the exact amount depends on your specific salary history and city.
Is a 10% annual raise good in Pakistan given inflation?
With headline inflation at 11.1% year-on-year as of June 2026, a flat 10% raise would actually be a slight real-terms pay cut, since prices rose faster than the raise. Whether a raise keeps pace depends on comparing it directly against the CPI figure for your specific review period, not a generic benchmark.
Which expenses have risen fastest for salaried Pakistanis?
Housing and utilities have been a particularly sharp pressure point, rising well above the headline inflation rate in recent readings, alongside elevated transport costs tied to energy prices. Because household budgets aren't evenly split, someone's personal inflation rate can run higher or lower than the national CPI average depending on their spending mix.
How do I calculate my real salary after inflation in PKR?
The standard approach divides your current salary by the cumulative CPI change since your reference period, giving the equivalent purchasing power in real terms. Doing this by hand across multiple years with fluctuating monthly rates is error-prone, which is exactly what a dedicated calculator is built to handle accurately.
